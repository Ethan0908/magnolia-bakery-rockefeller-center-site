# Website Brief

This is a sample/mockup website.

Business name: Magnolia Bakery - Rockefeller Center
Business type: bakery
City/location: Manhattan
Phone: Not provided
Website: Not provided
Address: Not provided

Goal:
Build a premium one-page website concept that looks good enough to send to the business owner as a sales sample.

Audience:
Local customers who need bakery services near Manhattan.

Required files:
- index.html
- style.css
- script.js

Design quality requirements:
- Make the page look modern, clean, and trustworthy.
- Use a strong hero section with a clear headline and call-to-action.
- Use professional spacing, strong typography, cards, subtle shadows, rounded corners, and good visual hierarchy.
- Use a consistent colour system.
- Make it look different from a basic school project.
- Do not make it look like plain default HTML.
- Make it mobile responsive.
- Add subtle JavaScript only if useful, such as smooth scrolling or small interactions.
- Do not use React, Next.js, Tailwind, Bootstrap, or build tools.
- Do not use external paid assets.
- Do not use fake testimonials with real names.
- Do not use lorem ipsum.
- Do not claim years of experience unless provided.
- Do not claim 24/7 service unless provided.
- Do not claim the business requested this.

Required sections:
1. Header with business name and navigation.
2. Hero section with headline, short value proposition, and call button.
3. Services or menu highlights section with 4 to 6 cards.
4. Why choose us section with trust-focused points.
5. Service area section mentioning Manhattan.
6. Contact call-to-action section with phone number and website if available.
7. Footer with this exact note: "Unofficial sample website concept."

Copywriting:
- Keep language clear and local.
- Use short sentences.
- Make the business sound professional.
- Avoid hype.
- Avoid generic phrases like "best in town" unless supported.
- For restaurants, focus on atmosphere, menu highlights, freshness, and reservations/orders.
- For trades, focus on reliability, fast response, clean work, and clear communication.

Final result:
The site should feel like a real homepage that could be used as a strong first draft for a paying client.


Rules:
- Use only static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
- Do not use React, Next.js, or build tools.
- Make it mobile responsive.
- Make it ready for Vercel.
